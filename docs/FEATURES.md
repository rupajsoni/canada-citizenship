# Northbound — Feature List

> Each entry: what the product does + what that unlocks for the user.
> The goal: a reader arrives at "I don't need anything else" without being told.

---

## Study flow

**Picks up where you left off**
No account. No setup. All progress saved locally. Close the tab, come back in three days — you're exactly where you were.

**Dashboard always surfaces the next move**
Not a menu of options. One CTA: *Continue lesson → [next module]*. The product decides what's next so you don't have to. No decision fatigue, no "where do I start?"

**Module quiz before you can call it done**
After the lesson and flashcards, a graded quiz. You don't self-report whether you learned it — the score tells you. You see it on the dashboard per module.

**10 modules, sequenced**
Structured in the order that builds comprehension — rights and duties → identity → history → government → economy. Not a dump of 200 facts.

---

## Practice and diagnosis

**Post-exam topic breakdown**
Every practice exam returns a per-category score (Rights, History, Government, etc.). You see exactly where you dropped points. You go back to the right lesson — not all of them.

**Readiness score**
Rolling average of your last 3 practice exams. Color-coded: red / yellow / green. Replaces "I think I'm ready" with a number. Updates every time you test. Tells you: *You are exam-ready* / *Almost there — review weak topics* / *Keep studying*.

**Exam history — last 12 runs stored**
You can see your improvement over time, not just feel it. Trend is visible.

**Practice exam mirrors the real test**
20 questions, timed, same pass mark (75%) as the official IRCC test. No format surprises on the day.

---

## Coverage

**Active recall flashcards per module**
Not re-reading. Flip → recall → check. Proven to outperform passive review. Every module has its own card deck.

**Interactive Canada map**
Provinces, territories, regions, Indigenous peoples, historical context. Geography questions on the real test require spatial memory — this builds it.

**Timeline drag-and-match drill**
12 key historical dates. Match year to event by tapping. Tests sequence memory, which passive reading doesn't.

**Built on the official *Discover Canada* guide**
Every fact traceable to IRCC source material. Not someone's interpretation. Not stale blog posts.

---

## Access and trust

**Free to use. No gate.**
Every feature, every result, every history entry is currently free. Pay-what-you-want support after you pass — by choice, not requirement.

**No account required**
Open it, use it. Zero friction. Works on mobile.

**Not affiliated with IRCC or the Government of Canada**
Independent. Accountable only to accuracy and user trust.
